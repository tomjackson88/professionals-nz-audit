Website Audit

Professionals New Zealand

professionals.co.nz Benchmarked against professionals.com.au February 2026
An audit of the NZ website against the signed-off AU site and Brand Guidelines V1.1. The site has adopted several elements of the 2025 rebrand well — the Ruby Red accent, the Charcoal base, and the italic headline treatment are all present on the homepage. However, there are consistency gaps, UX issues, and a few missed opportunities that should be addressed to bring it in line with the AU benchmark.

Homepage

Brand
01

The 29° Slash is Missing — Everywhere

No sign of the Professionals slash anywhere on the site. This is one of our most recognisable visual elements — born from the Open Door P — and it's used extensively across the AU site on property cards, hero imagery, and listing tiles. Its absence here makes the NZ site feel disconnected from the wider brand identity.

Same applies to listing cards — the AU site uses the slash crop on property photography. The NZ cards don't. Curious to know the thinking behind the omission.
Brand
02

Header Implementation

The AU site separates the logomark (P) and wordmark beautifully in the header — it's distinctive and gives the brand real presence. The NZ site uses a single combined logo image. Can we implement the AU header approach here?

UX
03

Search Form Feels Overwhelming

The search form on the NZ homepage exposes a lot of fields at once — region, district, suburb, property type, price min/max, beds, baths, cars, floor area, land area — all visible on load. Compared to the AU site's cleaner approach with progressive disclosure (simple bar + "Advanced" toggle), this feels cluttered and could be off-putting for casual browsers.

UX SEO
04

No Featured Properties on Homepage

No property listings visible on the homepage. The search form is up top, but there are no visuals or demonstration that properties are actively being listed and sold. The AU site has a prominent "Places We Love" section with tabs for For Sale, Sold, For Rent, and Leased. This is likely hurting SEO as well as first-impression engagement.

UX
05

No Stats or Social Proof Section

The AU site features a strong stats bar — settlements, total sales value, charity donations. This kind of social proof builds immediate trust and scale perception. The NZ site has no equivalent. Worth adding NZ-specific numbers to reinforce credibility.

UI
06

"How Can We Help" — Icons Don't Read as Buttons

The icon panel for Find an Office / Find a Salesperson / Find a Property Manager isn't immediately clear that these are clickable. Needs better hover states, visual affordance, and possibly a subtle background or border treatment to signal interactivity.

UI Brand
07

News & Insights Cards Need Work

A few issues with the article cards on the homepage:

Headlines feel too small — they don't command enough attention at the current size.
"Read More" button isn't flexible — when paragraphs are shorter, it leaves big awkward gaps beneath the text. The button should sit closer to the content.
Typography inconsistency — the News & Insights section uses Poppins for headlines, but the rest of the homepage uses Source Serif 4. This creates a wonky, disconnected feel between sections on the same page.
Blog on a separate subdomain — content lives at content.professionals.co.nz/blog, which is a separate subdomain from the main site. The AU site keeps blog content on the main domain. Flagging this for SEO — splitting content across subdomains can dilute domain authority.
UI
08

Subscribe CTA Panel

The subscribe/newsletter panel feels lost and underwhelming. There's an opportunity to make much more of this — a stronger visual treatment, clearer value proposition, and better placement could significantly improve conversion.

UX
09

Footer & Navigation Consistency

The footer needs some refinement — it feels a bit lacking overall. There's also a UX consideration around navigation grouping: the top nav dropdown for "About Us" includes contact links, but the footer separates "About" and "Contact" into distinct sections. So the same content is grouped differently depending on where you look. Just a small thing, but worth tidying up for clarity.

Social icons need hover states — currently no visual feedback on interaction.

About Us Page

UI
10

Header Legibility Issues

The heading and subheading in the page hero are hard to read. Likely a contrast or weight issue against the background. This is the first thing a visitor sees — needs to be sharp and clear.

UI
11

Irregular Section Padding

Inconsistent padding above and below the second section of the page. Creates an unpolished feel — spacing should be uniform and considered throughout.

Additional Findings

Brand
12

AI-Generated Imagery on Homepage

The homepage features images generated by Google Gemini — identifiable from the filenames (Gemini_Generated_Image_...). The Brand Guidelines are explicit on photography standards:

"The photography must feel modern, fresh, human, professional and approachable… must not feel staged… must not have any special effects, filters or colour grading."
— Brand Guidelines V1.1, Section 12

AI-generated people images can undermine the brand's principle of authenticity and trustworthiness. The AU site uses real photography throughout.

Action: At minimum, rename the image files so the AI origin isn't discoverable. Longer term, commission or source real photography that meets the guidelines.
